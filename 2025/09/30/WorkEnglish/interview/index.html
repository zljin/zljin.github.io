Q: Tell me about yourself

Hi, my name is phil.
I graduated from ABC University,major in Computer Science.
I have 5 years of experience as a software engineer
I am good at Java,SpringBoot.
And I also have a good understanding of DevOps, including Docker, Kubernetes.
I specialize in developing stable APIs for mobile banking systems.
A good example that comes to mind is when I developed CNY transfer and foreign exchange APIs at ABC.
Since release, these APIs have successfully processed many transactions for our application.
That’s all, Thanks.

Q: what are your weakness

I used to spend too much time on details, but now I manage it by setting priorities to deliver on time without compromising quality.

Q: why did you leave your last job

I really enjoyed these years that I worked in ABC,and during that time I’ve learned a lot and I met some amazing people.
To keep growing, I’m looking for new opportunities.

Q: sharing your project experience. please clearly explained the background, your role, and the outcome

Recently, I led the migration of our legacy mobile gateway to cloud-native.
The main reason for this was that our frontend team wanted to uplift angular to native apps.
However,the legacy mobile gateway have some issues with automatic logout across domains,and It is difficult to maintain them in the future.
Therefore,We had to demise it and reconstruct it on the cloud platform and Kong Gateway.

As the only API developer on the team, Here are some things that I did independently.
Firstly, I designed and implemented a proxy service using Spring Cloud gateway. And Integration in Kong Gateway.
Secondly, I upgraded all downstream services to support SAML 3.0 for DSP 3.0 integration.
Thirdly, I analyzed the current traffic and conduct the performance test.
Fourthly, I designed audit log to splunk that can make easily to monitoring.
Finally, I created pipeline and raised CRs to e2e production deployment.

During the project,I also coordinated closely with the frontend team to redesign API routes and Request Header.

In the end,we successfully migrated about 200 APIs from legacy mobile gateway to production.
This project helped me grow a lot in technical and coordination skills.

What was the biggest challenge you faced during this migration, and how did you overcome it?

On the technical side, the main challenge were implementing SSL context mutual for certain downstream services,
reducing the request size of headers, and fixing poor code quality in some legacy projects.

On the other side.
The biggest challenge was time management:
I had to coordinate with many front-end teams, troubleshoot issues quickly,
adjust parameters and modify downstream microservices once problems arose.
I also had to compete with other teams for hub environments switch.
To solve this, I prioritized the most critical tasks first, and documented all work details with Jira.
